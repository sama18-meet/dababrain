Neurotransmitters are the brains chemical messengers.

They are released at nerve terminals, diffuse across the synapse, and bind to receptors on the surface of the target cells.



Here, we will briefly discuss a few of the most common neurotransmitters:

  1. Acetylcholine

    This chemical is released by neurons connected to vouluntary muscles, causing them to contract, and by neurons that control the heartbeat. ACh is also a transmitters in many regions of the brain. Trouble with this neurotransmitter may couse fatigue and muscle weakness. In the brain it seems to be imortant for attention, memory, and sleep.


  2. GABA (gamma-Aminobutyric acid)

    The main inhabitoy neurotransmitter. (People who lack it might experience uncontrollable movement for example.


  3. Dopamine

    Exitetory neurotransmitter linked to movement, mental illnesses, and the endocrine system.


  4. Norepinephrine

    Plays a role in learning and memory. It is secreted by the sympathetic nervous system to regulate heat rate and blood pressure. It could be secreted either by sympathetc nerves or the adrenal medulla (the innermost part of the adrenal gland.


  5. Serotonin

    Important for sleep quality, mood, depression and anxiety. It also afects emotions. It is present in the lining of the digestive tract.


  6. Peptides

    Basically short chains of amino acids linked togetheer. This chemical is a pain killer
