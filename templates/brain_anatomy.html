The brain is divided into 3 major parts:

1. Forebrain


1. Cerebrm

The cerebrum is associated with activities such as thinking, perceiving, planning and understanding language.
The cerebrum is divided into two hemispheres the right hemisphere and the left hemisphere, which are connected by a bundle of fibers called the corpus callosum

.

Cerebral cortex (Gray matter):

The cerebral cortex is the outermost layer of the brain, most of it is folded into grooves, which increase the brains surface, allowing for inclusion of many more neurons. It is divided into 4 zones:

Frontal lobe:

Responsible for coordinating motor movement, higher cognitive skills, and for many aspects of personality and emotional makeup.

Parietal lobe

Sensory processes, attention and language. Its right side has to do with navigating spaces, and its left side is responsible for spoken/written language.

Occipital lobe

Visual information (shapes and colors)

Temporal lobe

Auditory information + integrating information from the other senses.

2. Basal ganglia (cerebral nuclei)

coordinate muscle movement

3. Thalamus

prioritizes and passes sensory information to cerebral coretex

4. Hypothalamus

Appetites and defensive behaviors


2. Midbrain

Consists of two pairs of small hills called colliculi play a critical role in visual and auditory reflexes.


3. Hindbrain

Pons and Medulla oblongata

Control respiration, heart rythms and blood glucose levels.

Cerebellum

Also has 2 hemispheres which help control movement, and cognetive processest that require percise timing.










4. Spinal cord

Recives sensory information and uses it for reflex responses. Also works through voluntary commands from the cerebrum.
